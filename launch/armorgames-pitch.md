# Armor Games — submission pitch

Armor Games does **not** have a self-serve uploader. You submit by **emailing `mygame@armorgames.com`**
with your game (a live link + a short pitch). They accept HTML5 now (Flash is gone). They favour games
with real depth — strategy, progression, replayability — which suits Port Boss. If they're interested
they'll reach out about featuring / a sponsorship or revenue deal. Non-exclusive is fine to state.

Keep it short and confident. Ready-to-send version below.

---

**To:** `mygame@armorgames.com`

**Subject:**
```
Game submission: Port Boss — HTML5 idle port-builder tycoon (free, mobile + desktop)
```

**Body:**
```
Hi Armor Games team,

I'd like to submit my game Port Boss for consideration.

Port Boss is a free, browser-based idle/tycoon game: you found a harbour on a wild coast and grow it from a small fishing village all the way to a neon-futuristic megaport. It's built in HTML5 (custom vanilla-JS + WebGL2, no plugins) and runs on both desktop and mobile.

It has the kind of depth your audience tends to stick with:
- A civilization timeline across eight ages that visibly remodels your harbour and your fleet as you advance
- Production chains with building synergies and per-port specialisation
- A fleet registry and navy you commission and evolve
- Trade routes between islands and timed expeditions
- Storms, pirate raids and construction you actually watch happen
- Endless prestige with a branching doctrine tree, relic sets, and a rival to race

It's a relaxed, generous design on principle: no energy timers, no pay-to-win, no loot boxes.

Play it here (live): https://ljennings11.itch.io/port-boss

It's non-exclusive and I'm happy to discuss featuring, sponsorship, or a build tailored to your platform (including your API/ads if you use one). I can also send a gameplay video, cover art, and a press-ready screenshot set.

Thanks for taking a look,
Lukas Jennings
lukasjennings11@gmail.com
```

---

### Notes for you
- Send from your own email; keep the live **itch link** front and centre — it's proof it works.
- If they reply asking for a build, send the **bare/no-SDK** zip (`itch/portboss-itch.zip`) or the
  gambling-free Poki build; they'll tell you if they want their own integration.
- Attach or link the **landscape cover** and **preview video** only if they ask — keep the first
  email light (a wall of attachments hurts deliverability).
- It's a relationship, not a form: a short, specific pitch beats a long one. One email is enough;
  don't chase.
